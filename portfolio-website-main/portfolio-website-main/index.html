I Have Created these Game Has traditional board game that has been enjoyed by players of all ages for generations. I have inspired By the game Pachisi game thata were played in india around the 6th century and has evolved into various versions across the world. These game is played on a square board with a cross-shaped track that consists of colored squares, and each player has four pieces of a distinct color. The objective of the game is to move all of one’s pieces from the starting area to the home area in the center of the board. Players take turns rolling a single die, with a roll of six being required to move a piece out of the starting area. The path on the board is dotted with "safe" squares where a piece cannot be sent back by an opponent’s piece. The gameplay involves both strategy and luck, as players must decide the best way to advance their pieces while avoiding having them "killed" or sent back to the starting area by an opponent who lands on the same square. The player who successfully moves all their pieces to the center first wins the game. Ludo’s simplicity and competitive elements make it a popular choice for family and casual play, and modern digital versions of the game have allowed it to remain a popular pastime globally.
